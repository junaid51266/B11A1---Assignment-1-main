Pillars of "Bangladesh 2.0"

Innovation

Innovation drives Bangladesh’s progress by fostering new ideas, solutions, and opportunities for sustainable growth and development.

Sustainability

Sustainability ensures the protection of Bangladesh’s environment, resources, and future through thoughtful and responsible development practices.

Infrastructure

Infrastructure development strengthens Bangladesh’s economy by enhancing roads, transportation, energy systems, and communication networks.

Empowerment

Empowerment equips the people of Bangladesh with skills, confidence, and opportunities to uplift their lives and contribute meaningfully to society.

The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation’s trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Digital Transformation

Creating a nationwide digital ecosystem to enhance connectivity and access to services for all citizens.

Green Energy

Investments in solar and wind energy to ensure a sustainable and eco-friendly future.

Modern Infrastructure

Building state-of-the-art infrastructure to support economic growth and global competitiveness.

"When I started Grameen Bank, I didn’t have any grand plans or visions. I simply wanted to solve a small problem — that poor people, particularly women, couldn’t access small loans from traditional banks. Over time, I realized this wasn’t just about finance; it was about creating a system that valued people over profit. Poverty, I believe, doesn’t belong in civilized society. It’s not created by poor people; it’s created by the systems we’ve built. If we redesign our systems to empower individuals, unleash their creativity, and provide opportunities, we can eradicate poverty completely. Every human being has unlimited potential, and by tapping into that, we can create a world of three zeros: zero poverty, zero unemployment, and zero net carbon emissions"

Dr. Muhammad Yunus

Chief Adviser of the People's Republic of Bangladesh
